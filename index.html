Different Encounters (“DE”) is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to the development of under-resourced, pre-adolescent youth in New York City. Through a basketball curriculum, DE positive engages young men in learning about themselves through education in a weekly, out-of-school atmosphere. This engagement will lead to better informed, more responsible young people who are ready to take on life, but more than that, ready to influence the next generation of young people through service leadership.

With its inaugural year in 2013 at the Mosaic Preparatory Academy, the Different Encounters program blends (i) physical education, (ii) character education and (iii) health education to provide students with guidance and support that will help them at school, at home and in their communities.

About Us

Ray Fredrick has been a NYC physical education teacher for over three years. Ray, a 9-year army veteran, has spent most of his time teaching in Upper Manhattan. He is concerned.

Due to a multitude of reasons, there is a lack of fundamental values amongst a large portion of young men living in troubled neighborhoods in New York City. There is a lack of:

  • Educational Focus
  • Community and Familial Responsibility
  • Respect and Self-Respect

There are few public and private organizations in NYC (particularly that can deliver a resonating message) to address these evident and ever-growing issues.

Ray Fredrick and Andrey Yoffe are dedicating their time, effort and money to teach and guide young men towards the core fundamentals of responsibility, community service leadership, education and family in an environment that facilitates the delivery of the message.

We have diagramed the following DE program:

  • Target audience overview:
    • Male students entering 5th or 6th grade

    • Length: ~2 ½ hours
    • Audience size: 15 – 25 students
    • Frequency: Once a week (weekdays)

    • Three 20-minute sessions of interactive discussion regarding health and character development

  • New York City Public Schools
