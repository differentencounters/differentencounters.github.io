Directed Energy (DE) is a new not-for-profit organization dedicated to the development of under-resourced, pre-adolescent youth in New York City.

Through a team sports curriculum, DE positively engages our young people in learning about themselves through education in a weekly, out-of-school atmosphere. This engagement will lead to better informed, more responsible young people who are ready to take on life, but more than that, ready to influence the next generation of young people through service leadership.

About Us

Ray Fredrick and Andrey Yoffe founded Directed Energy in 2013 to positively influence young men and women living in troubled neighborhoods in New York City.

We are dedicating the time, focus and resources to teach and guide young people towards the core fundamentals of (i) Responsiblity, (ii) Community Service Leadership, (iii) Education and (iv) Family in an environment that facilitates and sharpens the delivery of the message.

  • Educational Focus
  • Community and Familial Responsibility
  • Respect and Self-Respect

There are few public and private organizations in NYC (particularly that can deliver a resonating message) to address these evident and ever-growing issues.

Ray Fredrick and Andrey Yoffe are dedicating their time, effort and money to teach and guide young men towards the core fundamentals of responsibility, community service leadership, education and family in an environment that facilitates the delivery of the message.

There are few organizations that address these fundamental values in our young people. Please find more details on the Directed Energy program.

Our Partners

P.S. 50 Vito Marcantonio School

Principal: Cynthia Rochez

Grades: K – 8th (323 students)

Our second year as an organization and first year at P.S. 50 was another successful year for Directed Energy as we continued improving our curriculum and incorporating all the positive feedback we received from parents, students and faculty.

We held weekly sessions for 6th, 7th and 8th grade boys who immediately embraced Ray's coaching and tutelage. In addition to basketball skill and academic focus, parents and faculty also noticed heightened social responsibility and behavioral improvement among the students.

Mosaic Preparatory School

Principal: Lisette Caesar

Grades: PK – 5th (340 students)

Our inaugural year was a huge success as we self-funded a full year of weekly sessions for a group of 4th and 5th grade boys looking to further their basketball skills and strategies, while constantly opening up to DE program directors about personal achievements and difficulties.

Snow days on Tuesdays became the students’ nightmare, as the weekly afterschool sessions were highly coveted and anticipated.

Our Team

Ray N. Fredrick III

President and Program Director

Ray N. Fredrick III is a graduate of the United States Military Academy where he earned three letters as a member of the varsity men’s basketball team and served as a team captain during his senior year. After a successful five-year active duty military service to his country, Ray completed his master’s degree in physical education from Teachers College, Columbia University.

While at Columbia, Ray successfully established several afterschool physical activity programs including two highly successful jump rope programs at the The Nightingale-Bamford School and 92nd Street Y in Manhattan. In addition to after-school programming, Ray has served as a teacher in several New York City public schools with his latest position being as a physical education instructor at Teachers College Community School during the 2011-2012 school year. Currently, Ray is a physical education teacher in the Eastchester School District located in Westchester County, New York.

Andrey Yoffe

Vice President

Andrey A. Yoffe currently serves in an investment banking role at Barclays Capital, focused on the Aerospace and Defense industry. Andrey has been at Barclays since 2014 and has over 7 years of investment banking experience.

Previously, Andrey founded a local activities start-up company with a focus on stimulating activeness and healthy social interaction in people’s lives.

Andrey completed his bachelor’s degree in finance and international business from the Stern School of Business at New York University.

Risto Marttinen

Assistant Program Director

Risto Marttinen is a graduate of California State University, Fullerton where he received his Bachelors degree in Health Science and his Masters Degree in Kinesiology with an emphasis in Sports Psychology. He is currently attending Teachers College, Columbia University for his Doctorate Degree in Education in Curriculum and working as a physical education teacher at the Teachers College Community School in Harlem teaching Pre-K through 2nd grade physical education.

Risto’s background includes college wrestling for Fullerton’s Division I wrestling program where he was the Team Captain. Subsequently, he became head assistant coach where he was in charge of recruiting.

Risto brings to Directed Energy an understanding of sports psychology, teaching, coaching and an in-depth understanding of the college sports world.

Darnell C. Childs

Assistant Program Director

Darnell C. Childs, who helps lead the Directed Energy boys program, is a career educator and an active participant in the youth development NPO community. Darnell attended American International College on a basketball scholarship and later was a teacher at P.S. 256K and the St. Gabriel School.

In 2007, Darnell took on the role of program coordinator for Directions For Our Youth, a NPO that tends to at-risk students, particularly school drop outs. Shortly after, Darnell founded Helping Our Guys & Girls Succeed Inc., an organization focused on youth education through the completion of college.

Darnell joined Directed Energy in 2015 to further DE’s reach and development.
